## Known issues tracked but not blocking v0.1.0

- **Per-eye `CenterDepthSampler`**: exposure adaptation uses one shared running smoothed center depth across both eyes. Minor cross-eye exposure drift; not user-visible in typical scenes. Defer to v0.1.1.
- **Per-eye `SwapPass.targetTexture`**: Iris's swap-pass copies in `FinalPassRenderer` capture the target texture ID at construction time → ping-pong cross-eye color contamination during composite. Subtle; defer to v0.1.1.

## Architectural-fix dependencies

Several rows above had their expected results changed by 2026-05-17 architectural fixes:
- **E1** chunk-update counter direction inverted (now ~2× not ~half) — `e1870c0` rationale captured in commit body.
- **Shadow pass** correctness depends on `38f3644` (Task #21 — mono camera pos around shadow render) AND on skipping the shadow-texture clear in `IrisRenderingPipeline.beginLevelRendering` for the RIGHT eye (added 2026-05-18). The clear runs per `renderLevel` call, so without the skip, the second eye's pass wipes the LEFT eye's shadow map before sampling — symptom is zero shadows in RIGHT eye.
- **Geometry gaps** (sky color where geometry should be on RIGHT eye) was the symptom that triggered `e1870c0` (Sodium-skip removal). Re-verify on retest: no sky-color gaps on either eye with shaders ON.
